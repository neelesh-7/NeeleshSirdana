Posture Detection


Our program is pretty straightforward. It uses your camera system in your computer to calculate the distance between you and the camera using the parameters of your head and shoulders. If that distance is relatively far (if distance < 0.5), then you are not hunching over and your posture is good. When it is between a certain distance (if distance = 0.5), the video feed flashes yellow, giving you a warning that your posture needs to be fixed. If the distance is very minimal (very close to the screen and if distance > 0.5), the screen flashes red with lots of red text on the screen, and plays a very loud noise. This only happens after 1-2 minutes because we are aware sometimes you just want to see something close up or point at the screen to show something. You can see a detailed summary of your posture in Real-Time Plot. This creates a graph comparing distance with time. Using the parameters, it calculates the distance and tells you when you are slouching. This is a separate application that runs in the background.
